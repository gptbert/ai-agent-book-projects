**Account Executive** (AE) is a **full-cycle sales role** - essentially the person who owns the entire sales process from finding leads all the way through closing deals.

## What makes this AE role different from the SDR:

### **SDR vs AE at Alex:**

| **SDR** | **Account Executive** |
|---------|----------------------|
| Generates leads and books demos | **Owns the entire sales cycle** |
| Top of funnel | **Full funnel ownership** |
| Qualifies prospects | Sources leads, conducts demos, **negotiates, and closes deals** |
| Hands off qualified leads | **Closes the revenue** |

### **What this AE does:**

1. **Full-Cycle Sales** - You do it ALL:
   - Source your own leads (cold calls, emails, social, conferences)
   - Run product demos
   - Navigate procurement processes
   - Negotiate contracts
   - **Close deals** (this is the key difference!)

2. **Revenue Ownership** - Track and report on actual revenue generation, not just pipeline

3. **Sales Enablement** - Build the sales infrastructure (decks, templates, scripts)

4. **CRM Ownership** - Configure and manage the entire CRM setup

5. **Strategic Collaboration** - Work directly with founders on sales strategy and messaging

### **Key signals they're looking for:**

- **"Have you sold to recruiting leaders in HR, staffing, or RPOs?"** - They want someone with domain expertise
- **"How many six figure deals have you closed in the past 12 months?"** - This is a B2B enterprise sales role with large deal sizes

### **The Reality:**

This is an **early sales hire** (potentially the first AE) at a well-funded YC startup. You'd be building the sales function from scratch, carrying a quota, and directly responsible for revenue. Much more senior and autonomous than the SDR role - you own deals from start to finish and get compensated on closed revenue (hence the üí∞ emoji!).
